Exploring Japan’s Restaurants

ABOUT THE PROJECT

I love Japanese food

Japan is the country in the world with the highest density of restaurants/cafeterias in the world with one restaurant for every 266 people. The second place is for the United States with 566,020 and a density of one restaurant for every 547 people. (A Greek in Japan, 2010)

My friends will always ask me about food and restaurant recommendations for Japan since I lived in Tokyo for a couple of years and always loved the food and restaurant culture in Japan. It could be difficult to find a good restaurant in Japan as a visitor because most reviews and articles are written in Japanese. With this project, I’d like to provide useful information about where to find the best food in Japan through data visualization.

This project is implemented by using Python for data crawling, D3 for data visualization, and it is hosted on GitHub Pages.

Author Lacee Xu

ANALYSIS

Analysis of Ramen and Sushi Plots

Here are three pairs of graphs to show the analysis of some popular food categories.
The first pair is two histograms of the price range of the sushi and the ramen.
The second pair is two histograms of the rating distribution of the sushi and the ramen.
The last pair, two scatter plots shows the relationship between the price and the rating of the sushi and the ramen.




The Distribution of Ramen and Sushi Restaurant Prices

To those who have never spent any time in Japan, it might be hard to convey just how feverish the appetite for Ramen is over here. After Sushi, it’s probably the nation’s favorite.
It is clear that Ramen is cheaper in price range than Sushi when comparing the two histograms below. Most data of Ramen price range is from 500 to 1000JPY, 1000 to 1500JPY, while most data of Sushi price range vary from 500 to 8000JPY per person. But overall, I’d say most people end up spending around 1000 to 1500JPY per Sushi meal in Japan.




The Distribution of Ramen and Sushi Restaurant Ratings

We can see that Ramen has more data in a lower rating than Sushi when comparing the two histograms below. There’s still some Sushi restaurants have really high rating above 3.8.




The Correlation of Ramen and Sushi Restaurant Ratings and Price

From the pair of two scatter plots below, we can explore that the relationship between price and rating of Sushi is normally distributed - no matter expensive or not, you can get good Sushi. However, there are fewer Ramen restaurants with good ratings when the price is over 6000JPY, and more Ramen restaurants have ratings lower than 3.5 than Sushi restaurants in price range 1000JPY.
